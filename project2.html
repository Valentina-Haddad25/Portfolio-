AI-Driven
PCB Quality Assessment

Client:

Cadence Design Systems

Role:

Coder

Year:

2024

See Full Presentation

This project develops an advanced Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to assess the quality of Printed Circuit Board (PCB) designs by predicting their structural effectiveness. By leveraging deep learning, the model evaluates key design metrics, assigns a quality score, and identifies issues to streamline electronic design validation.

Objective:

To build an efficient CNN-based classification model that predicts the quality of PCB designs by analyzing signal crossings, congestion, and alignment. The goal is to automate the validation process, reducing manual errors and improving efficiency in electronic design.

Approach:

  • Dataset Creation: A simulated dataset of PCB designs with varying layouts and signal complexities.
  • Data Preprocessing: Resizing, normalization, and augmentation to enhance model generalization.
  • CNN Architecture: A multi-layered model with ReLU activations, dropout regularization, and pooling layers for optimal feature extraction.
  • Class Labeling: Binary classification (Good/Bad) based on the number of signal crossings.
  • Training & Optimization: Model trained with Adam optimizer, cross-entropy loss, and evaluated using confusion matrices.
  • Final Evaluation: Achieved 92% accuracy, validating its effectiveness in identifying PCB design flaws.

Impact & Future Work:

This AI-driven solution significantly reduces the time and cost required for PCB quality assessment while improving design reliability. Future enhancements will incorporate more complex quality metrics like congestion mapping and transfer learning for broader applicability in hardware engineering.
