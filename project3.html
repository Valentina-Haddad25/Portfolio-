As a Research Assistant at the Center for Computing & Data Sciences at Boston University, I spearheaded data collection initiatives across three Boston-based laboratories, focusing on comprehensive robot battery data analysis. My work aimed to uncover performance trends, energy efficiency metrics, and the impact of software/hardware configurations on battery longevity in robotics applications.

Objective:

The objective of this role was to develop robust data collection protocols and analytical models to better understand the dynamics of battery performance in autonomous robotic systems. The goal was to optimize battery usage, improve robot efficiency, and contribute to advancements in robotics energy management.

Approach:

  • Data Collection Leadership: Led data acquisition across multiple labs, designing and executing experiments that captured real-time battery performance under diverse operational conditions.
  • Cloud Data Integration: Engineered and implemented data gathering protocols using Azure SQL/NoSQL databases, ensuring efficient storage, scalability, and real-time accessibility of large datasets.
  • Experimental Robotics: Utilized my personal robot, Bittle, to conduct experimental trials, investigating correlations between battery consumption patterns and varying software algorithms and hardware configurations.
  • Collaborative Research: Partnered with PhD candidates to refine research methodologies, contributing data-driven insights that informed broader robotics projects. Worked closely with Professor Sabrina Neuman to enhance analytical frameworks and validate experimental outcomes.

Impact & Future Work:

This role enhanced my technical proficiency in robotics, data analytics, and cloud-based systems, while also strengthening my collaborative research skills. The findings from this research contribute to improving battery efficiency in autonomous systems. Future work will explore predictive modeling techniques to forecast battery degradation and integrate machine learning algorithms for autonomous energy management in robotics.
